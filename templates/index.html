How the Sentiment Analysis Works

The sentiment analysis system processes user reviews by following these steps:

  • Text Preprocessing: The input text is cleaned to remove unwanted characters, HTML tags, and non-alphabetic characters. This standardizes the text for further analysis, ensuring consistency in the data. For example, a review like "I love this phone!" is transformed into "i love this phone".
  • Feature Extraction: Key features are extracted from the cleaned text using techniques such as tokenization and vectorization. The TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) method is used to convert the text into a numerical representation that captures the importance of words in relation to the entire dataset.
  • Model Training: The extracted features are fed into a Logistic Regression model trained on a labeled dataset. In this case, the dataset consists of over 400,000 Amazon reviews of mobile phones, with ratings that indicate sentiment. Reviews with a rating greater than 3 are classified as Positive, while those with a rating of 3 or less are Negative.
  • Prediction: The trained model predicts the sentiment of the user's review based on the learned patterns from the training data. For instance, the input "Terrible battery life!" may be classified as Negative, while "Amazing camera quality!" is classified as Positive.

This system leverages Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to analyze and interpret the sentiment behind user reviews effectively. By understanding the sentiments expressed in customer feedback, businesses can enhance their products and services based on user preferences and experiences.

Dataset Information

The dataset used for training the model is the Amazon Unlocked Mobile dataset, which contains:

  • Over 400,000 reviews on various mobile phones.
  • Features including product names, ratings, review texts, and user feedback.
  • A diverse range of sentiments covering different user experiences, from highly positive to extremely negative.

Applications

This sentiment analysis system can be applied in various fields, such as:

  • Market Research: Understanding customer preferences and trends based on their reviews.
  • Product Improvement: Identifying areas for enhancement by analyzing negative feedback.
  • Brand Monitoring: Tracking public sentiment towards products or brands over time.
  • Customer Support: Automating responses to reviews based on sentiment classification.
